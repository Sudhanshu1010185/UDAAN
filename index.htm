About Udan

Udan (Ude Desh Ka Aam Nagrik) is a regional connectivity scheme launched by the Government of India in 2016. The scheme aims to make air travel affordable and accessible to citizens in regional areas by connecting underserved and unserved airports across the country.

Establishment in Bhubaneswar, Odisha:

In 2018, as part of the Udan scheme, Bhubaneswar, the capital city of Odisha, saw significant developments in its air connectivity. Multiple airlines, including Air Odisha and Air Deccan, operated flights under the Udan scheme to connect Bhubaneswar with various regional destinations within the state and neighboring states.

Key Points:

  • Udan flights from Bhubaneswar provided affordable air travel options to residents of Odisha and neighboring states, enhancing connectivity and promoting tourism and economic growth in the region.
  • The scheme led to the establishment of air routes connecting Bhubaneswar with smaller flight table and towns in Odisha, such as Jharsuguda, Rourkela, Jeypore, and others, which previously lacked direct air connectivity.
  • The introduction of Udan flights in Bhubaneswar also facilitated faster and more convenient travel options for business travelers and tourists visiting the region.

Impact:

The implementation of Udan in Bhubaneswar contributed to the overall development of the aviation sector in Odisha and played a vital role in boosting regional connectivity, economic development, and tourism in the state. It provided opportunities for residents of smaller towns and flight table in Odisha to access better healthcare, education, and employment opportunities in urban centers like Bhubaneswar, thereby promoting inclusive growth and development.
